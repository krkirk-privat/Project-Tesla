Tesla resale value

Depreciation is often the biggest item in a car's economics. Here's what affects a Tesla's resale value, and how to preserve as much of it as possible.

Last updated: June 2026

When you work out what a Tesla costs over time, depreciation is a bigger item than energy and service combined. So resale value is worth understanding, both if you're considering selling later, and if you're buying used.

What affects resale value?

FactorSignificance
Model and variantIn-demand models/variants often hold up better.
MileageFewer kilometres = higher price, all else equal.
Battery healthGood remaining capacity and warranty count positively.
ConditionPaint, interior and a damage-free history matter a lot.
Equipment and colourNeutral, popular choices are easier to sell.
The marketSupply and price changes on new cars affect used prices.

The key thing to know

The EV market moves faster than the classic car market, partly because new models and price changes affect used prices. So it's hard to promise specific percentages, treat resale value as something that depends on timing and market. On the other hand, you can do a lot to end up with an attractive car when it's time to sell.

How to preserve value

  • Regular cleaning and care keeps the car in good condition and pays off at sale.
  • Look after the battery: follow good charging practice, battery health is a key point at sale.
  • Keep documentation: service, repairs and warranty status make selling easier.
  • Protect the paint and interior from the start, and avoid unnecessary damage.
