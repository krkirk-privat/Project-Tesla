Tesla Model Y, complete guide

The Model Y is Tesla's spacious SUV and one of the best-selling cars in the world. Here's the overview of variants, range, space and charging, and who it suits.

Last updated: June 2026

The Tesla Model Y shares much of its technology with the Model 3, but packages it in a taller, more spacious SUV body. That gives easier entry, more boot space and a higher driving position, without compromising on range and charging speed. That's a big part of why the Model Y has become so popular with families.

The version on sale today is the facelift commonly called Juniper, with new front and rear styling, a calmer cabin and a softer ride. For the details of what changed, read the Model Y "Juniper" guide.

Space and practicality

The Model Y offers a large boot with extra under-floor storage, a frunk and a flat load floor when the rear seats are folded. The standard model seats five, while the Model Y L offers six seats. For many, it's exactly the space that makes the difference versus the Model 3.

Range and charging

The newest Long Range RWD variants are remarkably efficient and go far on a charge. The Model Y charges quickly on the Supercharger network (around 250 kW on supported variants), and day to day most owners charge at home. Who is the Model Y for?

The Model Y is the obvious choice if you want more space, a higher seating position and flexibility for family and leisure, while still valuing Tesla's efficiency and software. If you want a lower, slightly cheaper car, the Model 3 is the alternative.
