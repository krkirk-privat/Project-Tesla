Tesla Model 3 vs Model Y

The two most popular Tesla models share a lot of technology but are built for different needs. Here are the differences, and help choosing.

Last updated: June 2026

The Tesla Model 3 and Model Y are built on a shared platform and have much of their technology in common, the same software, the same approach to charging and many of the same components. The difference is mainly in body, space and seating position. Do you want a sporty sedan or a roomy SUV?

Space and practicality

The Model Y is the clear choice if space matters most. The taller SUV shape gives easier entry, more rear headroom and a larger, more flexible boot, and comes in a 6-seat Model Y L. The Model 3 is still roomy for a family, but is a classic sedan with a lower entry.

Range and efficiency

Both models are efficient, but the lower, lighter Model 3 typically has a small consumption edge. At the same time, the Model Y comes in very long-range Long Range RWD variants. In practice, the difference in everyday range matters less than the variant choice, compare the specific Model 3 variants and Model Y variants.

Price

The Model 3 is normally the cheapest entry into a new Tesla, while the Model Y costs a little more for the extra space. Since prices change and depend on variant and market, check current prices on tesla.com.

Driving and comfort

The Model 3 feels sportier and more direct thanks to its lower centre of gravity and weight. The Model Y gives a slightly higher, more relaxed seating position that many prefer for family driving and longer trips.

Which should you choose?

  • Choose the Model 3 if you want the cheapest entry, a sporty sedan and maximum efficiency.
  • Choose the Model Y if you prioritise space, a higher seating position and flexibility - or need six seats.
